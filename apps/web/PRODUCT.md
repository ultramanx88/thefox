# Product

## Register

product

## Users

theFOX serves Thai customers buying fresh local goods online, plus the vendor and operations teams who need a trustworthy production path for catalog, cart, order, and delivery workflows. Users are often comparing everyday grocery items quickly, checking stock and units, and expecting the interface to feel reliable enough for real orders rather than prototype browsing.

## Product Purpose

theFOX is a fresh local goods marketplace being migrated from an Expo/Firebase prototype into a production stack with Next.js, Fastify, PostgreSQL, Redis, shared Zod contracts, Docker deployment, and Cloudflare in front. Success means customers can confidently browse and order fresh goods while the team can scale vendors, roles, inventory, carts, orders, audit trails, and deployment operations without rewriting the foundation.

## Brand Personality

Modern, premium, fast, and operationally sharp. theFOX should borrow the mobile-first ordering clarity of LINE MAN and Grab, but its own brand language is black and silver rather than green: closer to Uber's restraint, with a polished metallic edge that fits a shiny silver fox-head mark. The tone can still feel local and useful, but the surface should feel like a serious delivery marketplace rather than a playful grocery app.

## Anti-references

Avoid green-led LINE MAN/Grab imitation, generic grocery templates that feel like stock ecommerce, over-decorated dark SaaS dashboards, neon cybermarket styling, glassmorphism, purple-blue AI gradients, and marketing pages that hide the actual catalog behind slogans. Do not make vendor/admin workflows feel playful at the cost of trust, density, or clarity.

## Design Principles

1. Make fresh goods concrete: product names, units, stock, delivery context, and vendor trust should be visible before decorative messaging.
2. Earn trust through operational clarity: actions, statuses, auditability, and validation states should be explicit.
3. Design mobile-first: the primary ordering flow should feel natural on a phone, with search, nearby shops, quick add, delivery ETA, and bottom navigation close at hand.
4. Keep theFOX black and silver: use green only when unavoidable for semantic success, never as the brand accent.
5. Preserve Thai readability: Thai copy needs generous line-height, clear hierarchy, and enough contrast in both light and dark themes.
6. Design for migration: favor patterns that can grow from mock data into real APIs, roles, carts, orders, and mobile clients.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast, keyboard access, visible focus states, and understandable form errors. Support both light and dark themes. Do not rely on color alone for status. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Keep Thai text legible on mobile, avoid cramped uppercase tracking for meaningful content, and make product cards usable at 320px width.
