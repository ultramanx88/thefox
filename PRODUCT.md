# Product

## Register

product

## Users

theFOX serves Thai customers who buy fresh goods and cooking ingredients, vendors with one or more branches who publish their own catalog and stock, drivers who accept delivery jobs, and operations/admin teams who keep orders, roles, and tenant boundaries under control. Users are often on mobile, comparing product units, freshness, price, stock, ETA, and delivery trust quickly.

## Product Purpose

theFOX is a multi-tenant delivery platform for ingredient-first commerce. Vendors can onboard their shops and branches, list products, receive orders, and coordinate delivery through a shared marketplace. Drivers can register and deliver orders like a standard delivery network. Customers can browse nearby fresh goods, authenticate once, and continue across web, PWA, and Expo mobile. Success means the platform can grow slowly and solidly across UX/UI, flow, business logic, security, and performance without returning to legacy Firebase dependencies.

## Brand Personality

Modern, premium, fast, and operationally sharp. theFOX should feel familiar to users of LINE MAN and Grab in ordering flow, but with its own black and silver identity rather than green. The visual posture is closer to Uber: restrained, confident, mobile-first, and polished enough to match the shiny silver fox-head logo.

## Anti-references

Avoid green-led LINE MAN or Grab imitation, generic grocery templates, playful vendor/admin tooling, neon cybermarket styling, glassmorphism, purple-blue AI gradients, and marketing-first pages that hide real catalog or workflow signals. Do not let decoration outrank product units, tenant ownership, delivery status, auth clarity, security, or operational trust.

## Design Principles

1. Make ingredients concrete: product name, unit, stock, vendor, branch, ETA, and delivery context should be visible before decorative messaging.
2. Design mobile-first, then scale up: customer ordering, auth, cart, status, and driver actions should feel natural on phone screens first.
3. Keep tenants explicit: vendor, branch, role, ownership, and permissions should be clear in both UI and backend flow.
4. Build trust through operations: state, validation, errors, auditability, and handoffs should be readable and predictable.
5. Keep theFOX black and silver: green can exist only as a semantic success signal, never as the brand accent.
6. Move slowly and solidly: UX/UI, flow, logic, security, and performance should evolve together rather than as isolated polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast, keyboard access, focus states, and understandable errors. Support light and dark themes. Do not rely on color alone for status. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Keep Thai copy legible on mobile with enough line height and contrast. Design auth and order flows so customers, vendors, drivers, and admins can recover from errors without losing context.
